Mime has played a crucial role in classical ballet for centuries. In more contemporary works, it has been replaced by facial and bodily expressions, but in the oldest and most traditional ballets, mime remains a fundamental part of the storytelling.

While some gestures are immediately obvious, others can easily go unnoticed. Understanding these movements is key to grasping what dancers are communicating on stage. The most recognized gestures can be found in ballets like *The Sleeping Beauty*, *Giselle*, *Swan Lake*, *Cinderella*, and many more from the Romantic era.

While these gestures have been fading from more modern choreography — where emotions are conveyed through freer movement — mime will endure as long as the classical ballets we love continue to be performed on stage.
